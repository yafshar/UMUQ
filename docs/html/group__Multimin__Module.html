Detailed Description

This is the Multidimensional Minimization Module of UMUQ providing all necessary classes for finding minima of arbitrary multidimensional functions.

Find a minimum in \( x=[0,1] \) of the interpolating cubic through \( (0,f0) \), and \( (1,f1) \) with derivatives \( fp0 \) at \( x=0 \) and \( fp1 \) at \( x=1. \)

The interpolating polynomial is:

\( c(x) = f0 + fp0 * z + \eta * z^2 + xi * z^3 \), where \( \begin{aligned} \nonumber \eta=3*(f1-f0)-2*fp0-fp1, \\ \nonumber xi=fp0+fp1-2*(f1-f0). \end{aligned} \)

Line search algorithm for general unconstrained minimization.

An assumption for this algorithm is that the line search can be restricted to the interval \( (0, \mu] \) where \( \mu = \frac{\bar{f}-f(0)}{\rho \acute{f}(0)} \) is the point at which the \(\rho-\text{line}\) intersects the line \( f = \bar{f} \).

After the bracketing phase has achieved its aim of bracketing an interval of acceptable points, sectioning phase generates a sequence of brackets whose lengths tend to zero.

In this algorithm \( \tau_1 > 1 \) is a preset factor by which the size of the jumps is increased, typically \( \tau_1 = 9 \). \( \tau_2 \) and \( \tau_3 \) are preset factors \( 0 < \tau_2 < \tau_3 \le \frac{1}{2} \).

Typical values are \( \tau_2 = \frac{1}{10} \) ( \( (\tau_2 \le \sigma \text{is advisable}) \) and \( \tau_3 = \frac{1}{2} \), although the algorithm is insensitive to the precise values that are used.

Recommended values from Fletcher: \( \rho = 0.01, \sigma = 0.1, \tau_1 = 9, \tau_2 = 0.05, \tau_3 = 0.5 \)

Ref: R. Fletcher, Practical Methods of Optimization (Second Edition) Wiley (1987), ISBN 0471915475.
